About SproutScribe

We’re a student research team from Raffles Institution (RI) exploring sustainable writing instruments as a secondary school science project.

Our mission: reduce the plastic footprint of disposable pens by designing a pen that writes smoothly, biodegrades safely, and can be planted to grow into a plant at end-of-life.

Tagline: “stories today, a greener tomorrow.”

Team & mentor with early prototypes.

Innovative Features

100% Biodegradable Body

Plant-based blend (PLA + bamboo fiber) designed for durability during use and composting.

Seed Capsule in Cap

Non-GMO seeds enclosed for planting after use.

Smooth, Safe Ink

Vegetable-based pigments tuned for flow and stability, comparable to normal ballpoints.

Scan & Learn More

QR concept for planting tips and learning resources.

FAQ

Any questions?

Yes — we compiled every question received and answered them below.

NA / nil?

Those were treated as “no question”.

How to open/close pen?

Twist or click prototypes; both avoid metal springs to keep parts compostable.

Where’s the cap?

The cap is a seed capsule — moisture-protected but easy to open for planting.

How much will it cost?

Target: mid-range pen pricing; bulk purchases keep costs down.

How long will the pen last?

Similar to standard ballpoints. Biodegradation starts in composting conditions, not in your bag.

Where’s the grip?

Textured bamboo-fiber sleeve; we’re testing shapes for comfort.

What if I don’t want the seed?

Choose a seed-free cap or pass the seed to a friend/community garden.

Would this cost more than a normal pen?

Materials are slightly higher, but bulk + refills make total cost comparable.

What’s the point of the seed in the pen?

Turn disposal into learning: plant, track germination, and reflect on circular design.

“Look at overall writing solution so we can save paper… digital transfer?”

We agree — we’re exploring QR-linked resources, recycled notebooks, and smarter note-taking to reduce paper use.
