The Science Behind SproutScribe

Materials Research

We developed a proprietary blend of PLA (polylactic acid) derived from corn starch, reinforced with bamboo fibers. The ink is based on vegetable pigments for safety and biodegradability.

Decomposition Timeline:

0–2 months: 15% degraded

2–4 months: 60% degraded

4–6 months: 92% degraded

Testing Methodology

Controlled decomposition tests were carried out in three environments: industrial compost, home compost, and landfill conditions. Optimal degradation occurred in compost settings.

  • No toxic residues left after decomposition
  • Seed germination success: 78%
  • Writing performance equal to conventional pens

FAQ – Your Questions, Answered

How to open/close the pen?

Prototype A uses a twist mechanism; Prototype B uses a click top. Both are designed without springs to keep parts biodegradable.

Where’s the cap / grip?

We’re testing a minimal cap that doubles as a seed holder. A textured bamboo-fiber sleeve provides grip without extra plastic.

How much will it cost?

Target retail is similar to mid‑range pens. Bulk school pricing is possible as materials scale.

How long will the pen last?

Comparable to a standard ballpoint in normal classroom use. It begins to break down only in composting conditions.

What if I don’t want the seed?

You can gift the seed, plant it in community gardens, or choose the seed‑free version we’re testing.

Does this cost more than a normal pen?

Material costs are slightly higher, but classroom multipacks and refills bring the total cost close to regular pens.

What’s the point of the seed in the cap?

It turns end‑of‑life into a classroom activity—plant the cap and track germination while the pen body biodegrades.

Why choose this over a cheap pen?

This pen reduces plastic waste and teaches circular design. It writes smoothly and adds an educational component.

“Look at overall writing solution… digital transfer?”

We agree! We’re exploring paper‑light workflows: QR‑linked content, recycled notebooks, and refillable ink—all alongside SproutScribe.
